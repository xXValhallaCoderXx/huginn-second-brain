Huginn keeps a personal knowledge base for you. As you chat, it quietly captures things worth remembering — a preference you mentioned, a decision you made, a fact you shared. You can also ask Huginn to save something explicitly, search and browse everything it knows about you, and create or edit notes directly from the dashboard.

The same knowledge base is accessible everywhere: notes saved via Telegram show up on the web, and notes you create on the dashboard are available when you're chatting on Telegram.

## Automatic knowledge capture

During normal conversation, Huginn notices when you share something meaningful — a preference, a goal, a decision, a recurring fact about your life — and saves it without interrupting the flow. When it does, it briefly confirms: **"Noted — saved [title] to your knowledge base."**

You don't need to do anything for this to happen. Just chat naturally and Huginn builds up your knowledge base over time.

### Smart consolidation

Huginn doesn't just pile up separate notes for every new thing you mention. Before creating anything new, it searches your existing knowledge base and decides whether the new information belongs inside a note that already exists.

For example, if you once told Huginn "my favourite language is Rust" and you later say "I also really enjoy TypeScript," Huginn will **update** the existing note — broadening it to cover both — rather than creating a second, nearly identical note. The result is a cleaner, more useful knowledge base that grows richer over time instead of cluttered.

<Callout type="info">
  Huginn is selective about what it captures. It saves things that are likely to be useful later,
  not a transcript of everything you said. When it updates an existing note, a revision is recorded
  so nothing is ever lost.
</Callout>

## Asking Huginn to remember something

If you want to make sure something is saved, just tell Huginn directly:

- "Remember that I prefer morning meetings."
- "Save this: my emergency contact is Jordan, +1-555-0123."
- "Note that we decided to go with the blue colour scheme."

Continuing a topic works too — if you follow up with more details on something you previously mentioned, Huginn treats that as explicit context to save and will update the relevant note automatically.

Huginn confirms with a short acknowledgement:

- **New note:** "Noted — saved [title] to your knowledge base."
- **Updated note:** "Updated your note on [title] with the new information."

## Recalling saved knowledge

You can ask Huginn about your knowledge base in natural language at any time:

- "What do you know about my diet preferences?"
- "Do you have anything saved about my home network setup?"
- "What did I tell you about the project deadline?"

Huginn uses **semantic search** to find the most relevant notes — meaning it understands the meaning of your question, not just the exact words. Asking "what are my tech preferences?" will surface a note titled "Programming Language Preferences" even though those exact words don't appear in your question.

## The Knowledge Base page

The **Knowledge Base** page in the dashboard gives you a full view of everything that's been saved. Reach it from the **Knowledge** link in the top navigation bar.

At the top of the page, a stats line shows you at a glance how many notes and connections are in your knowledge base — for example, **"4 notes · 1 connection"**.

### Searching and filtering

Use the search bar and filter controls to find notes quickly:

- **Search by keyword** — type any word or phrase to filter notes by title and content.
- **Filter by source** — use the **All**, **Saved by Me**, or **Captured by Huginn** pills to narrow by who created the note.
- **Filter by tag** — click any tag in the tag cloud to show only notes with that tag. Click **Apply Filters** to confirm your selection.

You can combine a keyword search with source and tag filters at the same time.

### Creating a note manually

<Steps>
<Step>
### Open the Knowledge Base page

Click **Knowledge** in the top navigation bar.
</Step>
<Step>
### Click New Note

Click the **New Note** button in the top-right corner. A blank note form will appear.
</Step>
<Step>
### Fill in the details

Enter a **title** and the note **content**. You can optionally add **tags** to help organise and filter your notes later.
</Step>
<Step>
### Save the note

Click **Save** to create the note. It will appear in your note list immediately.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Editing a note

Click on any note to open it in inline edit mode. You can update the title and content directly. You will be asked to provide an **edit reason** — a short description of what you changed. This reason is stored in the note's revision history so you can always see why a note evolved.

Click **Save** when you're done.

### Deleting a note

Each note has a **Delete** button. Clicking it will show an inline confirmation step so you don't accidentally remove something. Confirm to permanently delete the note.

<Callout type="warn">
  Deleted notes cannot be recovered. If you're unsure, consider editing the note to mark it as
  outdated rather than deleting it.
</Callout>

## Dashboard preview

The main dashboard shows a **Saved Knowledge** card displaying your note count and connection count — for example, **"4 notes · 1 connection"**. This gives you a quick snapshot of your knowledge base without navigating away from the dashboard.

## Understanding note metadata

Every note shows:

| Field              | What it means                                                                                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Title**          | A short label for the note                                                                           |
| **Content**        | The full text of the note                                                                            |
| **Tags**           | Colour-coded pills for filtering and organisation                                                    |
| **Captured by**    | A bookmark icon means you created it manually; a sparkle icon means Huginn captured it automatically |
| **Revisions**      | A badge (e.g. "1 rev") appears on notes that have been updated — showing how many times they changed |
| **Date**           | When the note was first created                                                                      |
| **Source channel** | Whether the note came from the web dashboard or Telegram                                             |

### Connections

Notes can be linked to related notes, forming a small knowledge graph. The stats line in the page header counts how many connections exist across your knowledge base. A full Graph view is planned for a future release.

## Cross-channel access

Your knowledge base is tied to your Huginn account, not to any specific channel. Notes created or captured during a Telegram conversation are immediately visible on the web dashboard, and notes you create on the web are available when Huginn responds in Telegram.

<Callout type="info">
  You can start a thought in Telegram and continue it on the web — Huginn has the full context
  regardless of where you're chatting.
</Callout>
